# Risk and Governance Executive Committee 

## Purpose of Charter 

The RGEC Charter sets out the role, composition, and responsibilities of the Risk and Governance Executive Committee (“RGEC”) of Graphene. 

## Scope 

This Charter Covers all of Graphene. 

## Purpose of the RGEC Committee 

The purpose of the Risk and Governance Executive Committee (“RGEC”) of the Board of Directors (the “Board”) of Graphene shall be to assist the Board of Directors of Graphene in its oversight of Graphene’s risk governance structure, including Graphene’s risk management framework and policies, procedures, and practices utilized to manage risk. The RGEC has the authority: 

 To investigate any matter brought to its attention with full access to all books, records, facilities, and personnel of Graphene. To retain independent consultants to advise the RGEC, at Company expense, as it deems necessary. Graphene shall provide for appropriate funding, as determined by the RGEC, for the payment of compensation to consultants or advisors employed by the RGEC and ordinary administrative expenses of the RGEC that are necessary or appropriate in carrying out its duties. 

## Risk Committee Team 

The Risk and Governance Executive Committee shall be appointed by and will serve at the discretion of the Board. The Chairman of the Risk and Governance Executive Committee shall be a member of the Board. 

## ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES 

The Risk and Governance Executive Committee shall periodically assess risks to the effective execution of business strategy and review key leading indicators in this regard. The key roles and responsibilities of the committee shall be as follows: 

 Review the risk management processes and practices of Graphene and ensure that Graphene is taking the appropriate measures to achieve a prudent balance between risk and reward in both ongoing and new business activities. Evaluate significant risk exposures of Graphene and assess management's actions to mitigate the exposures promptly (including one-off initiatives and ongoing activities such as business continuity planning and disaster recovery planning and testing). Evaluate risks related to cybersecurity and ensure appropriate procedures are placed to mitigate these risks promptly. Prepare and submit reports to the Board, including reports concerning risk management and minimization procedures. Review and reassess the adequacy of this Charter periodically and recommend any proposed changes to the Board for approval. Formulate and delegate authority to subcommittees when appropriate.

## Meeting 

The Committee shall meet at least 2 times per year. 

## Reporting 

Proceedings of all meetings are minuted and signed by one member of the Committee. Minutes of all Committee meetings are circulated to Committee members and at the start of each subsequent meeting, the status of action items identified at the end of the previous meeting is discussed. 

## REVIEW OF CHARTER 

The Board of Directors will review, update, and approve this charter annually to ensure it remains consistent with the Committee’s objectives and responsibilities.
