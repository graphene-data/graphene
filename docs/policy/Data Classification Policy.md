# Data Classification Policy 

Data classification is the process of assigning value to information in order to organize it according to its risk to loss or harm from disclosure. 

## Purpose 

This policy assists Graphene staff in determining what information can be disclosed to outside parties, as well as the relative sensitivity of the information that should not be disclosed outside of Graphene without proper authorization. 

## Scope 

This policy covers all information assets owned and managed by Graphene including (but not limited to), information (electronic and non-electronic), associated IT infrastructure such as software, networks, desktops, laptops, servers. Further, this policy is applicable to the owners, custodians, and all users (employees, directors, shareholders, consultants and contractors) of such information assets. 

## High Level Policy 

All information assets (electronic and non-electronic) shall have designated owners and be classified in accordance with information classification guidelines stated in this document. All Graphene employees, directors, shareholders, consultants, and contractors who handle information stored at or controlled by Graphene are responsible for understanding and implementing this policy. Where a third party will be responsible for handling the information on behalf of the Graphene, the third party shall be required by contract to adhere to this policy prior to the sharing of information. 

## Detailed Policy 

### Asset Identification 

All information and related IT assets of Graphene shall be clearly identified and have an owner. This includes information assets, physical IT assets, and IT services. 

### Asset Classification 

Graphene shall classify, record and maintain an inventory of information assets. The asset inventory shall include a list of all information assets owned and operated by Graphene including, but not limited to, information in an electronic and non-electronic form. Graphene shall maintain an inventory of all information assets including details on asset ownership, classification and location. The asset inventory listing shall be reviewed and updated by management on an annual or as-needed basis. 

# Information Classification 

Information in a "final" or published state that is either in the custody of or produced and owned by Graphene must be classified into one of the following four categories: **Public** — Information that is not confidential and can be made public without any implications for the organization. Such information is available to the public, employees, consultants and contractors without any authorization. **Internal** — Information that is available to employees and authorized non-employees (consultants and contractors) possessing a need-to-know for business-related purposes. **Confidential** — Information that is sensitive within Graphene and is intended for use only by specified groups of employees. A breach of such information could cause serious embarrassment and possibly undermine public trust in the organization. 


**Restricted** — Information that is extremely sensitive and is intended for use by named individuals or positions only. A security breach would risk the health, safety, privacy, or reputation of an individual, members of the public, Graphene, and its Personnel or Customer organizations. In some circumstances, confidential or restricted information might have to be disclosed to outsiders such as statutory auditors, external consultants, regulatory and legislative bodies, etc. The asset owner shall use their discretion with responsibility in getting NDAs signed from such outsiders. Even after such disclosure, the classification still remains "Confidential" or "Restricted" and does not become "Public."

## Asset Labeling and Handling 

To ensure that the correct controls are applied to the information assets of the organization, a system of protective marking is used i.e., for printed reports, use of watermarks, warning signs for screen displays, classification level for emails, etc. Any information which is not explicitly classified is classified as Confidential by default to avoid data leakage. Information and its related IT assets are processed and stored strictly in accordance with the classification levels assigned to those assets. All information assets must be secured to meet the requirements of their respective classification levels. Access to the information assets shall be the responsibility of a designated owner. All information classified as Internal or Confidential must have security controls applied which are sufficient to ensure that the information is accessible only to those users who are authorized for access. Any information, which needs to be disclosed or published outside Graphene (to the media, press, etc.) shall be done explicitly by or through corporate communications and based on authorization. For each security classification level, a set of handling controls must be in place to ensure that the information asset involved is appropriately protected at all times.
