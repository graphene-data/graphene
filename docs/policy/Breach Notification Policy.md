# Breach Notification Policy 

## Purpose 

This policy outlines the procedures for identifying, reporting, and managing breaches of Protected Health Information (PHI) at Graphene. It aims to ensure compliance with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) Breach Notification Rule and to safeguard the privacy and security of PHI. 

## Scope 

This policy applies to all employees, systems, and processes involved in handling or disclosing PHI within Graphene, including those operated by Business Associates (BAs) on our behalf. 

## Definitions 

Secured PHI: PHI that is rendered unusable, unreadable, or indecipherable to unauthorized individuals through HHSapproved methods such as encryption or destruction. Unsecured PHI: PHI that is not protected by HHS-approved methods and is vulnerable to unauthorized access. Breach: An impermissible use or disclosure of PHI under the Privacy Rule that compromises the security or privacy of PHI. Exceptions include: Unintentional acquisition or access in good faith. Inadvertent disclosure to an authorized person within the same organization. Good faith belief that the unauthorized recipient could not retain the PHI. 

## Policy 

Graphene is committed to protecting PHI and will handle breaches in accordance with HIPAA requirements. This policy provides guidelines for the timely and effective management of breaches. 

## Discovery and Reporting of Breach 

A breach is deemed discovered on the first day Graphene is aware or should have been aware of it through reasonable diligence. Employees must promptly report any suspected breaches to the Privacy Officer or their designee. The Privacy Officer will initiate an investigation, assign an investigator, conduct a risk assessment, and determine the necessary notifications. 

## Internal Reporting 

Immediate reporting of a potential breach to the Privacy Officer or designee is required. The report must include all relevant information such as names, dates, nature of the breach, and involved personnel. Discussions about the breach should be confined to the Privacy Officer or designee. 

## Investigation 

Upon notification, the Privacy Officer or designee must conduct an investigation which includes interviews, documentation collection, and appropriate record-keeping. All related documentation must be retained for a minimum of six years. 

## Breach Determination 

A breach is presumed unless Graphene can demonstrate a low probability that PHI has been compromised. 


Risk assessment factors include: Nature and extent of the PHI involved. Unauthorized person's access to the PHI. Actual acquisition or viewing of the PHI. Mitigation efforts taken. The Privacy Officer or designee will make the final determination and decide on further actions, including external notifications. 

## Notification Requirements 

### Notification to Individuals (Applicable to Covered Entities Only) 

If the Privacy Officer or their designee decides that notifying individuals is necessary, they must draft and send a breach notification to each individual whose unsecured PHI has been, or is reasonably believed to have been, accessed, acquired, used, or disclosed due to the breach. The notice must be clear and written in plain language. If it is known that the individual has passed away and the address of their next of kin or personal representative is available, the notice should be sent to that next of kin or representative. If contact information is outdated or insufficient, a substitute notice is not required. The notification must be issued promptly, and in no case later than 60 calendar days following the discovery of the breach. 

**Notification to the Media (Applicable to Covered Entities Only)** For breaches involving unsecured PHI that affect more than 500 residents of a state or jurisdiction, Graphene must inform prominent media outlets in that state or jurisdiction. This notification must occur without unreasonable delay and no later than 60 calendar days after the breach is discovered. The notification may be issued as a press release. 

**Notification to the Secretary (Applicable to Covered Entities Only)** Following the discovery of a breach of unsecured PHI, Graphene must notify the Secretary of the Department of Health & Human Services (HHS). The appropriate notification form can be found on the HHS website: . 

HHS Breach Notification Form 

If the breach impacts 500 or more individuals, Graphene must notify the Secretary within 60 days of discovering the breach. For breaches affecting fewer than 500 individuals, notification to the Secretary can be included in an annual report, which must be submitted no later than 60 days after the end of the calendar year in which the breaches were discovered. 

**Notification to Covered Entity (Applicable to Business Associates Only)** If a breach of unsecured PHI occurs at or through a business associate, the business associate must promptly notify the covered entity. This notification must be made without unreasonable delay and no later than 60 days after the breach is discovered. The business associate must provide the covered entity with information including the identities of affected individuals and any other details needed by the covered entity for its own notifications to those individuals. If the Privacy Officer or designee concludes that notification to the covered entity is warranted, they must quickly prepare and send the notice to the covered entity. 

**Delay of Notification for Law Enforcement** If a law enforcement official requests a delay due to an ongoing investigation or national security concerns: Written requests must specify the delay period and be honored. Oral requests must be documented and the delay must not exceed 30 days unless a written request is received. 

**Documentation** All documentation related to the breach, including logs, risk assessments, and notifications, must be retained for a minimum of six years. 

**Review and Updates** This policy will be reviewed and updated periodically to ensure continued compliance with HIPAA regulations and organizational needs.
